# :mountain: Bedrock

The postulate is one line, $S^1 = \partial(\text{Möbius}) \hookrightarrow S^3$ with $\partial S^3 = \emptyset$, and it has two sides. These are the standalone mathematics papers underneath it, each proving one side as a result in spectral geometry with no physics assumed: the boundary that carries time, and the closed space it is embedded in. The bridge between them is the embedding itself.

## Pillars

**[First eigenvalue](first-eigenvalue.md)**: the $S^1$ side. The twisted Laplacian on a constant-curvature Möbius band, where the orientation twist and the collapsed-fiber cone fix the first positive eigenvalue at $2/R^2$ while leaving the ground state extension-dependent. That eigenvalue is what the framework reads as the cosmological constant $\Lambda$.

**[Coexact gap](coexact-gap.md)**: the $S^3$ side. Flat bundles on the spherical space forms $S^3/\Gamma$, where the coexact spectral gap is the first McKay-occurrence level: $4/R^2$ for every irreducible flat connection across the ADE classification, with one exception, the Galois connection on $S^3/2I$ at $36/R^2$. That gap is the Yang-Mills mass gap.

## Bridge

*Forthcoming.* The embedding $\partial(\text{Möbius}) \hookrightarrow S^3$: the template that seats the Möbius band of the first pillar, whose boundary is its $S^1$, as a totally geodesic surface inside the $S^3/2I$ of the second, tying the two spectra to one postulate.
